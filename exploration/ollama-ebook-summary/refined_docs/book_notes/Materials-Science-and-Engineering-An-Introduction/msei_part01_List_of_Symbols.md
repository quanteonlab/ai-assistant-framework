#### Structure Levels in Materials
Background context explaining the different levels of structure in materials, ranging from subatomic to macrostructure. Each level is defined based on the size and complexity of structural elements.

:p What are the five levels of material structure?
??x
The five levels of material structure are:
1. **Subatomic Structure**: Involves electrons within individual atoms, their energies, and interactions with nuclei.
2. **Atomic Structure**: Relates to the organization of atoms to yield molecules or crystals.
3. **Nanostructure**: Deals with aggregates of atoms that form nanoparticles having nanoscale dimensions (less than about 100 nm).
4. **Microstructure**: Structural elements observable using a microscope, with dimensions between 100 nm and several millimeters.
5. **Macrostructure**: Structural elements visible to the naked eye, with scale ranges from several millimeters to on the order of a meter.

:p What are some microscopic techniques used to investigate atomic structure, nanostructure, and microstructure?
??x
Microscopic techniques commonly used to investigate atomic structure, nanostructure, and microstructure include:
- **Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM)**: For imaging surface features at high resolution.
- **Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM)**: For observing internal structures of materials with atomic-level detail.
- **Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM)**: For mapping topographical surfaces on a nanoscale.

These techniques allow for detailed examination and analysis of the structural elements at various scales, providing insights into the properties and behaviors of materials.
