- It lacks the force of enacted law and only offers persuasive authority.
- Its statements often represent the majority view but may not reflect the settled law in all jurisdictions.
- Some states follow minority views even when the Restatement states them.

:p What are the key limitations of the Second Restatement of Contracts?
??x
The key limitations include its status as a non-enacted document, thus lacking legal force and only providing persuasive authority. It often represents the majority view but may not accurately reflect settled law in all jurisdictions due to varying judicial interpretations. #### Freedom of Contract Principle
Background context explaining the freedom of contract provision as a fundamental principle within UCC Article 1, allowing parties to agree on different rules.

:p What is the "freedom of contract" provision in UCC Article 1?
??x
The freedom of contract provision in UCC Article 1 (Section 1-302(a)) allows parties to vary or modify the provisions of the Uniform Commercial Code through their agreement. This means that contractual terms can deviate from default rules set by the code, as long as such variations are agreed upon by the contracting parties.

For example, in North Carolina, Section 25-1-302(a) provides:
```pseudocode
§ 25-1-302. Variation by agreement.
    (a) Except as otherwise provided in subsection (b) of this section or elsewhere in [the Uniform Commercial Code],
        the effect of provisions of [the Uniform Commercial Code] may be varied by agreement.
```
This means that unless specifically prohibited, parties can negotiate and agree on terms different from those set forth by the UCC.

#### Parol Evidence Rule
Background context: The parol evidence rule determines which unwritten terms to include if the agreement is part written and part oral.

:p According to the parol evidence rule, under what circumstances can written contracts be supplemented with oral agreements?
??x
The parol evidence rule generally prevents parties from introducing extrinsic (outside) evidence that contradicts or modifies a clear and complete written contract. However, it allows for the inclusion of unwritten terms if:
1. The written contract is incomplete.
2. There is an ambiguity in the writing.

Thus, oral agreements can be used to clarify ambiguities but cannot contradict the explicit terms of the written contract.

A party can claim they were not in breach of contract in several scenarios:

#### Remedies for Breach of Contract
Background context: If a breach of contract occurs, the non-breaching party is entitled to remedies. These include expectancy (position as if the contract was performed), reliance (costs incurred based on the expectation of performance), and restitution (returning what was received).

:p What are the main types of remedies available for a breached contract?
??x
The main types of remedies available for a breached contract are:
1. **Expectancy:** Restores the non-breaching party to the position they would have been in had the contract been performed.
2. **Reliance:** Compensates the non-breaching party for costs incurred based on reliance on the contract's performance.
3. **Restitution:** Requires the breaching party to return any benefits received from the non-breaching party.

For example, if Party A breaches a contract by failing to deliver goods, Party B can seek expectancy damages (to receive what was promised), reliance damages (costs incurred in preparation for delivery), and possibly restitution (return of any payments made).
