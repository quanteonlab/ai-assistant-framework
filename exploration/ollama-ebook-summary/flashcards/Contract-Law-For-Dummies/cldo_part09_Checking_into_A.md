#### Concept of Exculpatory Clauses
Exculpatory clauses are provisions in contracts where one party agrees not to hold another party liable for certain acts or omissions. These clauses are commonly found in residential leases but can appear in various other agreements.

An exculpatory clause is a legal provision that absolves one party from liability for specific actions, such as negligence. For example, a tenant might agree not to hold the landlord responsible for injuries sustained due to negligent maintenance of common areas.

#### Evaluation of Exculpatory Clauses
Courts often grapple with whether to enforce exculpatory clauses, balancing freedom of contract against discouraging negligent behavior. The evaluation involves several factors such as clause breadth, notice, bargaining power, and economic implications.
:p What are the common concerns about exculpatory clauses?
??x
Common concerns include:
- **Breadth**: Whether it exculpates from simple negligence or more serious acts like intentional torts.
- **Notice**: If the clause is in fine print, the other party might not have known it was part of the contract.
- **Bargaining Power**: In situations where one party holds significant power (e.g., scarce housing), the agreement might be seen as unfair.
- **Economics**: Who should bear the risk of loss and who has more control over avoiding such costs.
