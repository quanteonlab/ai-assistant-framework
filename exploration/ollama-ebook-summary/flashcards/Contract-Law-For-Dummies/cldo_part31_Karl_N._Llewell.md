#### Karl N. Llewellyn
Background context: Karl N. Llewellyn was a leading legal scholar and professor at Columbia University and the University of Chicago, best known for his contributions to the Uniform Commercial Code (UCC). He is associated with the legal realist movement, which viewed law as a social science interlinked with psychology, sociology, and anthropology.

Relevant information: His work includes influential pieces such as "The Cheyenne Way" (1941), which influenced his drafting of the UCC. His writing style can be challenging to read, but he introduced significant ideas in legal theory.

#### E. Allan Farnsworth
Background context: E. Allan Farnsworth was a professor at Columbia University and the principal Reporter for the Second Restatement of Contracts (1981). He is known for his comprehensive treatise, "Farnsworth on Contracts," which remains one of the most readable works in contract law.

Relevant information: Farnsworth's work did not focus on innovation but was notable for its synthesis and clear expression. His treatise continues to be a valuable resource for students and practitioners alike.
