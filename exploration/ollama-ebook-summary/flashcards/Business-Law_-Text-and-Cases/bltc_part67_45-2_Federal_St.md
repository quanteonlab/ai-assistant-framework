#### Federal Regulations on Water Pollution - Early Legislation
Federal regulations governing water pollution can be traced back to the 1899 Rivers and Harbors Appropriations Act. This act prohibited ships and manufacturers from discharging or depositing refuse in navigable waterways without a permit. In 1948, Congress passed the Federal Water Pollution Control Act (FWPCA), but its regulatory system and enforcement powers proved to be inadequate.

:p What was the purpose of the Rivers and Harbors Appropriations Act?
??x
The Rivers and Harbors Appropriations Act aimed to prevent ships and manufacturers from discharging or depositing refuse in navigable waterways without a permit. It established initial regulatory measures for water pollution control.

---
#### The Clean Water Act (CWA)
In 1972, Congress passed amendments to the FWPCA, creating the Clean Water Act (CWA). The CWA had three main goals: making waters safe for swimming, protecting fish and wildlife, and eliminating the discharge of pollutants into the water. It also set specific schedules for limiting pollutant discharges.

:p What were the primary objectives of the Clean Water Act?
??x
The primary objectives of the Clean Water Act (CWA) were to make waters safe for swimming, protect fish and wildlife, and eliminate the discharge of pollutants into the water. The act also established schedules for limiting these discharges based on available technology.

---
#### Permit System for Point-Source Emissions - NPDES
The CWA established a permit system called the National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (NPDES) to regulate discharges from "point sources" of pollution, such as industrial, municipal, and agricultural facilities. These permits ensure that any discharge will not violate water quality standards.

:p What is the purpose of the NPDES system?
??x
The purpose of the NPDES system is to regulate discharges from point sources (industrial, municipal, and agricultural facilities) to ensure they do not violate water quality standards. This helps control pollution and protect aquatic environments.

---
#### Elements of the NPDES Permit System
The NPDES permit system includes several elements: national effluent standards set by the EPA for each industry, state-established water-quality standards under EPA supervision, a discharge permit program that sets water-quality standards to limit pollution, special provisions for toxic chemicals and oil spills, and construction grants and loans from the federal government for publicly owned treatment works.

:p What are the key components of the NPDES system?
??x
The key components of the NPDES system include national effluent standards set by the EPA for each industry, state-established water-quality standards under EPA supervision, a discharge permit program that sets water-quality standards to limit pollution, special provisions for toxic chemicals and oil spills, and construction grants and loans from the federal government for publicly owned treatment works.

#### Best Available Control Technology (BACT)
Regulations generally specify that the best available control technology (BACT) be installed. BACT is the most effective combination of technologies that are achievable at reasonable costs.

:p What does the term "Best Available Control Technology" refer to?
??x
The term "Best Available Control Technology" (BACT) refers to the most effective combination of technologies that can be achieved at a reasonable cost. This ensures that the highest level of pollution control is implemented where necessary, while being practical and economical.
