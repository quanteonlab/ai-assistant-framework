#### Appraisal Rights
Background context: If a shareholder disapproves of a merger or consolidation but is outvoted, they have a statutory right to be paid the fair value of their shares. This right is known as the appraisal right.
:p What happens if a shareholder disagrees with a merger or consolidation and loses in a vote?
??x
If a shareholder disagrees with a merger or consolidation but loses the vote, they are entitled to receive the fair market value of their shares from the corporation. The "fair value" typically refers to the value on the day prior to the vote.
This ensures that even if a shareholder does not want to be part of the new entity resulting from the transaction, they can still exit by receiving a fair price.

#### Poison Pill Defense
Background context explaining the poison pill defense. The right is triggered when a party acquires a certain proportion of the target corporation’s stock—often between 15 and 20 percent. This tactic aims to make a takeover too expensive for the acquiring party by diluting their interest through new share issuance.
:p What does the poison pill defense do?
??x
The poison pill defense works by giving shareholders the right to acquire newly issued stock if any individual acquires more than a certain percentage of the company's shares, typically between 15 and 20 percent. This increases the number of shares outstanding, thereby diluting the acquiring party’s interest.
