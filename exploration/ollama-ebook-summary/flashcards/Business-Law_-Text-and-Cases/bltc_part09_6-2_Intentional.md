1. **Trespass to Personal Property**: The wrongful possession or use of another person's personal property without just cause.
2. **Conversion**: The invasion of another’s real property without consent or privilege, once a person is expressly or impliedly established as a trespasser.

For example:
- Trespassing on someone's land without permission would be considered a trespass to personal property.
- Using someone else's car for an unauthorized purpose could be seen as conversion if the owner did not give explicit permission.

- In a case of trespass to personal property, the court would need to determine whether the defendant entered the property without justification.
- In a case of conversion, the court would assess whether the defendant used or took possession of the property in a manner that violated the owner's rights.

#### Unintentional Torts—Negligence
Background context: The tort of negligence occurs when someone suffers injury because of another’s failure to live up to a required duty of care. In contrast to intentional torts, negligence does not require the defendant's intent.

:p What is the tort of negligence and how does it differ from intentional torts?
??x
The tort of negligence occurs when someone suffers an injury due to another party's failure to meet their duty of care. Negligence differs from intentional torts in that it does not require the defendant's intent or maliciousness.

For example:
- If a driver fails to stop at a red light, causing an accident, they would be negligent.
- However, if someone intentionally rammed another car to cause damage, that would be considered an intentional tort like assault or battery.

??x
To establish negligence, the plaintiff must prove four elements:
1. **Duty**: The defendant owed a duty of care to the plaintiff.
2. **Breach**: The defendant breached this duty by failing to act as a reasonable person would under similar circumstances.
3. **Causation**: The breach caused the plaintiff's injury or damages.
4. **Damages**: The plaintiff suffered actual harm as a result of the breach.

```java

    private Victim victim;

            // If the driver failed to stop at a red light, causing an accident.
            victim.injuredDueToCollision();
        }
    }

    public boolean determineNegligence() {
        // Determine if the driver's failure to stop caused the accident and injury.
        return true; // Assuming the breach of duty resulted in injury.
    }
}
```
