#### Case in Point: Estray Statute Application

Explanation of a landmark case establishing finders' rights under estray statutes.

:p How did the court rule in the Armory v. Delamirie case?
??x
In the Armory v. Delamirie case, two railroad employees found a duffel bag containing $165,580 in cash and reported it to U.S. Customs agents. The federal government filed a lawsuit claiming title to the property under criminal forfeiture laws because of its involvement in illegal drug transactions. However, the two employees argued that they were entitled to the money under Maine’s estray statute.

The court ruled that the employees had not fulfilled their obligations under the estray statute requirements: (1) providing written notice to the town clerk within seven days after finding the property, (2) posting a public notice, and (3) advertising in the town's newspaper. Because they did not meet these requirements, they had not acquired title to the property.

Therefore, the U.S. government had a right to seize the cash.

#### Definition of Bailment
A bailment is formed by the delivery of personal property, without transfer of title, by one person (called a bailor) to another (called a bailee). The distinguishing factor between a bailment and a sale or gift is that possession is transferred while title remains with the bailor.
:p What is a bailment?
??x
A bailment occurs when personal property is delivered from one party (the bailor) to another (the bailee) without transferring ownership. The bailee has temporary control over the property, but the owner retains legal title.
x??

---
#### Elements of a Bailment
For a transaction involving the delivery of property to become a bailment, three elements must be present:
1. Personal property.
2. Delivery of possession (without title).
3. Agreement that the property will be returned or otherwise disposed of according to the owner’s directions.

These elements ensure that there is an agreement between parties regarding temporary control and eventual return or disposition of the property.
:p What are the three essential elements for a bailment?
??x
The three essential elements for a bailment are:
1. Personal property: The item must be movable, not real estate or a person.
2. Delivery of possession (without title): The bailee must have exclusive control over the property and knowingly accept it.
3. Agreement: There should be an understanding that the property will be returned or disposed of in accordance with the bailor's instructions.
x??

#### Physical versus Constructive Delivery
Physical or constructive delivery can result in the bailee having exclusive possession of and control over the property. For a bailment to be established, either type of delivery must occur.

Example: A coat checked at an airport forms a bailment because it has physical possession. However, if there is a valuable item inside that goes unnoticed by the bailee, no bailment exists for that item.
:p What distinguishes physical from constructive delivery in a bailment?
??x
Physical delivery involves handing over tangible property to another party with exclusive control and custody. Constructive delivery involves giving someone the power or authority to use the property, which can be inferred as acceptance.

In both cases, the bailee must have sole possession of the property:
- Physical Delivery: Directly handing over an item.
- Constructive Delivery: Giving someone legal rights or instructions that effectively result in control over the item.

Example:
```java

        // Physical Delivery example

            System.out.println("Physical bailment created");
        } else {
            System.out.println("No physical bailment created");
        }
        
        // Constructive Delivery example
        DiamondNecklace necklace = new DiamondNecklace();
        Bag bagWithContents = new Bag();
        if (bagWithContents.contains(necklace)) { // Assume this method checks contents
            System.out.println("Constructive delivery assumed, but no bailment created");

            System.out.println("No constructive bailment created due to unknown contents");
```
