#### Adverse Possession Doctrine
The adverse possession doctrine allows a person to acquire legal ownership of property by continuously occupying and using it for a statutory period, typically 10-20 years. This doctrine serves several public-policy reasons:
1. Resolving boundary disputes
2. Determining title when in question
3. Ensuring real property remains in the stream of commerce
4. Punishing owners who do not take action against adverse possession and rewarding possessors for productive use.

:p What is the purpose of the adverse possession doctrine?
??x
The primary purposes are to resolve boundary disputes, clarify titles in uncertain cases, ensure land stays in active use, and penalize idle property ownership while incentivizing productive use. This doctrine balances the interests of current occupants and original owners.
