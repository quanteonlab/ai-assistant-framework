#### Business Scenario 3 - Adverse Possession

Background context: The McKeag family has used a section of their neighbor's property as a beach for more than forty years, claiming it as part of their marina. However, they did not have formal permission from the Finleys.

:p Discuss whether the McKeags can claim adverse possession over the beach.

??x
The McKeags may potentially be able to claim adverse possession over the beach under certain conditions if they meet the legal requirements for such a claim. Adverse possession typically involves five elements: possession, continuity of possession, exclusivity, open and notorious use, and the statutory period (usually 15-20 years in many jurisdictions).

Given that the McKeags have used the property continuously for over forty years, this meets the requirement of both continuity and duration. Their use was also likely exclusive since they prevented others from using it, including their neighbors. Additionally, their actions were open and notorious as evidenced by them building structures there.

However, a key point is that one member of the Finley family gave permission for the McKeags to continue using the beach. This could potentially invalidate any claim to adverse possession if the Finleys retained legal ownership throughout this period.
