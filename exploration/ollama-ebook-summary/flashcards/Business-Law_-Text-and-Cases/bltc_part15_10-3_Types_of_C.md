#### RICO Criminal Provisions
Background context: The Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act (RICO) is a United States federal law that provides for prosecution of organizations involved in racketeering activities, such as organized crime. It imposes severe penalties on individuals who use a pattern of certain predicate crimes to acquire or maintain power, control, or profit.

Criminal penalties include fines up to $25,000 per violation and imprisonment for up to twenty years, with the possibility of both being applied concurrently. Additionally, any assets related to illegal activities are subject to government forfeiture.

:p What is RICO and what does it aim to address?
??x
RICO targets organized crime by allowing prosecution of individuals involved in a pattern of racketeering activity, which includes violations of various federal offenses such as fraud, extortion, and murder. It seeks to dismantle criminal organizations through significant financial penalties and the seizure of ill-gotten assets.
x??

#### Exclusionary Rule
Background context: Under the exclusionary rule, any evidence obtained in violation of constitutional rights (Fourth, Fifth, Sixth Amendments) generally cannot be admitted at trial. Evidence derived from illegally obtained evidence is known as the "fruit of the poisonous tree" and must also be excluded. The purpose of this rule is to deter police misconduct.
:p What is the purpose of the exclusionary rule?
??x
The purpose of the exclusionary rule is to deter law enforcement from conducting warrantless searches and engaging in other forms of misconduct that violate constitutional rights. By excluding evidence obtained through such violations, the rule aims to ensure that only legally obtained evidence can be used in court, thereby upholding the integrity of judicial proceedings.
