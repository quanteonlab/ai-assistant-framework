#### Case Precedents and Stare Decisis
Background context: In the king’s courts, judges attempted to make their decisions consistent with previous decisions, called precedents. This practice gave rise to the doctrine of stare decisis, which obligates judges to abide by these established precedents in their jurisdictions.
:p What is the purpose of the doctrine of stare decisis?
??x
The purpose of the doctrine of stare decisis is to ensure consistency and predictability in legal decisions by requiring judges to follow previous rulings (precedents) when making new judgments. This practice helps maintain uniformity in the application of law across similar cases.
x??

#### Legal and Equitable Remedies
Background context: Legal remedies involve monetary or tangible compensation, while equitable remedies involve non-monetary relief such as specific performance or injunctions. Courts can choose between these based on the nature of the case and the relief sought.
:p What are legal and equitable remedies?
??x
Legal remedies include payment of money or transfer of property to compensate for a wrong. Equitable remedies involve actions like specific performance, injunctive relief, or rescission of contracts that do not involve monetary payments.

Background context: An age-old question in law is whether a "bad" law must be obeyed if it violates fundamental moral principles. The natural law school explores this concept, questioning the legitimacy of laws that conflict with inherent human rights or ethical standards.

??x
The central issue addressed by the natural law school is whether a law that contradicts fundamental moral or ethical principles should still be obeyed. This approach questions the legitimacy of laws based on their alignment with what are considered inherent human rights and values.

#### Federal Powers
Explanation that the U.S. Constitution explicitly lists certain powers that can be exercised by the federal government, along with implied powers necessary to carry out those explicit duties. The remaining powers are reserved for the states.
:p What does the U.S. Constitution primarily establish regarding the distribution of power between the national and state governments?
??x
The U.S. Constitution divides power by explicitly listing specific (enumerated) powers for the federal government, while reserving all other powers to the states or the people through the Tenth Amendment.

#### Regulatory Powers of States
Explanation that state governments have inherent sovereignty allowing them to regulate certain affairs within their borders under police powers. This authority is derived from the Tenth Amendment and encompasses a wide range of activities to protect public welfare, health, safety, etc.

??x
Police powers allow state governments to regulate private activities for the protection or promotion of public order, health, safety, morals, and general welfare. This includes enacting laws such as fire and building codes, antidiscrimination laws, zoning restrictions, etc.
