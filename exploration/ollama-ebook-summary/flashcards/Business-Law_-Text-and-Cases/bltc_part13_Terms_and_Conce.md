#### CAN-SPAM Act Overview
Background context: The Controlling the Assault of Non-Solicited Pornography and Marketing (CAN-SPAM) Act was enacted by Congress in 2003 to address unsolicited commercial e-mail. This legislation affects businesses and individuals sending such emails.

The act applies to any “commercial electronic mail messages” sent to promote a product or service. It preempts state anti-spam laws, except for provisions that prohibit false and deceptive emailing practices. Generally, the act allows the sending of unsolicited commercial email but prohibits certain types of spamming activities.

:p What is the CAN-SPAM Act?
??x
The CAN-SPAM Act is a federal law in the United States enacted to regulate unsolicited commercial emails (spam). It requires senders to include valid contact information, provide an option for recipients to opt out of future emails, and prohibits false or misleading header information.

Key points:
1. Covers commercial electronic mail messages.
2. Allows sending but regulates activities like using false return addresses and dictionary attacks.
3. Preempts most state anti-spam laws except those related to deceptive practices.
??x
The answer with detailed explanations.
```plaintext
The CAN-SPAM Act is a significant piece of legislation aimed at controlling unsolicited commercial emails. It requires senders to include valid contact information, such as an accurate email address and physical postal address. Additionally, recipients must be provided with the option to opt out of receiving further emails from the sender. The act also prohibits the use of misleading or false header information in emails.
```
