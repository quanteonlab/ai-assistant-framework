#### Property and Its Protection
In property law, a bailee can be held liable for injuries resulting from the bailment of defective items. The primary responsibility lies with the bailor to ensure that leased property is fit for its intended purpose.

:p What does liability mean in the context of property protection?
??x
Liability refers to the legal obligation of one party (in this case, the bailee) to compensate or make amends for harm caused due to negligence or breach of duty. In bailment law, it means that if a leased item is defective and causes injury, the bailor must be held responsible.

#### Warranty Liability for Defective Goods
Warranty liability arises when goods provided under a lease are found to be defective. The goods must be suitable for their intended use regardless of whether the bailor had knowledge or could discover any defects.

:p What determines if property is fit for its intended purpose in a lease agreement?
??x
Property leased from a bailor must be fit for the specific purpose agreed upon by both parties. This determination does not depend on the bailor's actual knowledge or ability to detect any defects; it’s based solely on whether the property meets the needs of the lessee.

#### Common Carriers and Their Duties
Common carriers, such as public transportation services, are required to carry all passengers or freight under specific conditions. They operate under a strict liability standard when handling goods in bailment.

:p What is the difference between common carriers and private carriers?
??x
Common carriers are publicly licensed to serve the general public with transportation services. They must carry all passengers or freight as long as there is enough space, fees are paid, and no reasonable grounds exist for refusal. In contrast, private carriers can operate only for a select clientele and may choose whether to provide service based on their discretion.

x??

#### Strict Liability Applies
Strict liability means that common carriers are absolutely liable for all loss or damage to goods transported by them, except in cases of natural disasters or war. This standard is higher than the reasonable care standard used by ordinary bailees.

:p What does strict liability mean for common carriers?
??x
Strict liability implies that a common carrier must bear responsibility for any loss or damage to the property they are transporting, irrespective of their level of care or negligence. The only exceptions are natural disasters or acts of war, which are beyond the control of the carrier.

x??

#### Limitations on Liability
While common carriers cannot contract away their liability entirely, they can limit their financial responsibility through contractual agreements and government regulations. They must adhere to stated limits for insuring shipments.

:p How do common carriers limit their liability?
??x
Common carriers may set a maximum amount of insurance coverage per shipment in the terms of service. This limitation is subject to governmental regulations but allows carriers to manage risk more effectively by setting a specific dollar cap on their liability.

#### Case Study: UPS and Liability Disclaimers
In certain cases, common carriers like UPS provide pre-defined disclaimer clauses that limit or completely disclaim liability for items exceeding their stated value. Courts may enforce these disclaimers if all terms are clearly communicated to the shipper.

:p What did the court decide in the T reiber & Straub case?
??x
The court ruled that UPS’s disclaimer of liability for packages valued above $50,000 was enforceable despite the shipper’s purchase of additional insurance. This decision highlights how strict contract terms can limit a carrier's liability when such terms are clearly communicated and agreed upon by both parties.

#### Real Property (or Realty)
Real property, also known as realty, includes land and everything permanently attached to it. This encompasses structures, fixtures, airspace, sub-surface rights, plants, and vegetation. Essentially, real property is immovable.
:p What does real property include?
??x
Real property includes the land itself, any buildings or structures on that land, other permanent attachments like fixtures (such as appliances), airspace above the land, underground resources including minerals, oil, natural gas, and even plants and vegetation.
x??

#### Land and Structures
Land consists of the soil and its natural products or artificial structures attached to it. It also includes water bodies on or under the surface and much of the airspace above it. The exterior boundaries of land extend down to the center of the earth and up to the farthest reaches of the atmosphere, with some exceptions.
:p What does land include?
??x
Land includes the soil, any natural products or artificial structures attached to it, water bodies (both on and under the surface), a significant portion of airspace above the land. The boundaries extend down to the center of the earth and up to the farthest reaches of the atmosphere.

#### Airspace Rights
The owner of real property has rights over both the airspace above the land and the subsurface. These rights can be limited by encumbrances, which must typically be indicated on the document transferring title at purchase. Disputes regarding airspace rights may involve commercial or private plane flights, cloud seeding for artificial rain production.
:p What rights does a real property owner have over airspace?
??x
A real property owner has the right to both the airspace above their land and the subsurface beneath it. These rights can be limited by encumbrances that must usually be documented during a title transfer. For example, commercial or private planes flying over a property could interfere with the owner's enjoyment of the land if the flights are frequent and low.

#### Subsurface Rights
In many states, ownership of land can be separated from subsurface rights. The surface owner can sell these rights to another person without requiring the consent of the subsurface rights holder. These rights include minerals, oil, or natural gas. However, a subsurface rights holder cannot use the surface to exercise their rights.
:p Can subsurface and surface rights be separate?
??x
Yes, in many states, ownership of land can be separated into surface and subsurface rights. The surface owner can sell the subsurface rights to another person without the consent of the other owner. This separation allows for a more flexible use of property but ensures that the subsurface rights holder cannot use the surface to exercise their rights.

#### Real Property Rights are Not Absolute
Real property rights are never absolute; they can be limited by various encumbrances such as easements or zoning laws. The nature of real property ownership is complex, involving multiple legal considerations.
:p Are real property rights always absolute?
??x
No, real property rights are not always absolute. They can be limited by encumbrances like easements, zoning laws, and other legal restrictions. These limitations ensure that the use of one's property does not negatively impact others.
