The primary procedural difference lies in their objectives:
- **Action at Law**: Seeks monetary damages or specific performance.
- **Action in Equity**: Aims to prevent unjust acts, such as granting injunctions or ordering restitution.

These differences affect the types of remedies available and the court's jurisdiction.

#### How to Read Citations
Legal citations typically include a reporter abbreviation, volume number, page number, and case name. Understanding how to read these citations is crucial for accessing legal opinions accurately.

:p What do the components of a legal citation represent?
??x
A typical legal citation includes:
- **Reporter Abbreviation**: Identifies the publication source (e.g., U.S. for United States Supreme Court).
- **Volume Number**: Specifies the edition and sequence number.
- **Page Number**: Indicates where the case begins in the volume.
- **Case Name**: The parties involved in the litigation.

Example: "U.S. 123, 456" refers to page 456 of Volume 123 in the U.S. reporter series.

#### Courts of Equity
Background context: When individuals could not get adequate remedies in court of law, they petitioned the king for relief. Advisers to the king, called chancellors, granted new and unique remedies. Formal chancery courts or courts of equity were eventually established to handle these cases.
:p What is the main difference between a court of law and a court of equity?
??x
The main difference lies in their approach to resolving disputes: Courts of law deal with monetary damages (remedies at law), while courts of equity focus on providing remedies based on justice and fair dealing, such as specific performance or injunctions.

Specific performance involves ordering a party to fulfill an agreement as promised; an injunction is an order to cease engaging in a certain activity or undo a wrong; rescission cancels a contractual obligation.
:p What are some examples of equitable remedies?
??x
Examples of equitable remedies include:
- Specific performance: Ordering a party to perform their promise.
- Injunction: An order to stop doing something or to correct a wrongdoing.
- Rescission: Cancellation of a contract.

#### Stare Decisis
Background context: The doctrine of stare decisis requires courts to adhere to precedent in order to promote predictability and consistency. In this case, the Supreme Court had to decide whether to follow the outdated Brulotte decision or overturn it.
:p How does the concept of stare decisis apply to the Kimble v. Marvel Entertainment case?
??x
Stare decisis applies by requiring courts to adhere to precedent in order to maintain legal consistency and predictability. In this case, the Supreme Court had to decide whether to uphold the outdated Brulotte decision or overturn it based on current economic understandings.
x??
