#### Act of State Doctrine
Background context: The Act of State Doctrine is a principle that U.S. courts will avoid passing judgment on the validity of public acts committed by a recognized foreign government within its own territory. This doctrine is designed to respect the sovereignty of other nations and their ability to manage internal affairs.
:p What does the Act of State Doctrine protect?
??x
The Act of State Doctrine protects the sovereign rights of foreign governments to act as they see fit within their own territories, preventing U.S. courts from interfering with such acts unless they are deemed illegal by international law or treaties.

Background context: Sections 1605A and 1610(g) of the Foreign Sovereign Immunities Act (FSIA) provide specific conditions under which a foreign state's immunity can be waived. :p What did the U.S. Supreme Court conclude regarding Section 1610(g)?
??x
The U.S. Supreme Court concluded that Section 1610(g) does not provide a freestanding basis for parties holding a judgment under Section 1605A to attach and execute against the property of a foreign state where the immunity of the property is not otherwise rescinded under a separate provision within Section 1610. This decision aligns with the FSIA's intent to carefully balance respect for sovereign immunity with the ability to seek redress in certain cases.

#### Sherman Act Application
Background context explaining the application of the Sherman Act. The act covers any conspiracy that has a substantial effect on U.S. commerce, and can apply even if the violation occurs outside the United States. Foreign governments and businesses can be sued for violations, but jurisdiction is only exercised if there's a substantial effect on U.S. commerce.
:p What does the Sherman Act cover in terms of its application?
??x
The Sherman Act covers any conspiracy that has a substantial effect on U.S. commerce, regardless of where the violation occurred. It applies to both businesses and foreign governments, but jurisdiction is only granted if there's a significant impact on U.S. commerce.
