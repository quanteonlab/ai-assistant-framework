#### Fossils and Their Use in Paleontology
Background context: Fossils are preserved remains or traces of ancient life forms that provide insights into past environments, ecosystems, and evolution. Some fossils occur in clusters, indicating communal living or traveling behavior. Amber can trap and preserve organisms, offering a snapshot of prehistoric conditions.

Scientists use various methods to date fossils:
- **Radiometric Dating**: Involves observing the natural decay of radioactive elements.
- **Relative Dating**: Evaluates the layers of sediments and rocks; different layers and types are older or younger than others.
- **Paleomagnetism**: Compares magnetic particles in sediment layers to known worldwide shifts in Earth's magnetic field.
- **Biochronology**: Dates fossils by comparing them to other well-dated species.

:p What dating method involves observing the natural decay of radioactive elements?
??x
Radiometric dating is a method that scientists use to date fossils. It involves measuring the decay rate of radioactive isotopes within the fossil material, which helps determine its age relative to when it formed.
```java
// Pseudocode for Radiometric Dating
public class RadiometricDating {
    private double halfLife;
    private double initialAmount;

    public double calculateAge(double remainingAmount) {
        return (Math.log(remainingAmount / initialAmount) / (-1 * (halfLife / Math.log(2))));
    }
}
```
