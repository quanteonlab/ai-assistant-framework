#### Card 3: Joint Types in the Human Body
Background context explaining the seven major types of joints (hinge, ball and socket, pivot, fixed, saddle, condyloid, gliding) with their functions.

:p List and describe the seven major types of joints found in the human body.
??x
1. **Hinge Joints** - These work like a door hinge or book binding, allowing movement in one direction (e.g., elbows and knees).
2. **Ball and Socket Joints** - Found in shoulders and hips, these allow for wide range of motion due to the ball fitting into a bowl-shaped socket.
3. **Pivot Joints** - Permit rotation around another bone, like in your neck’s first and second vertebrae (C1 and C2).
4. **Fixed Joints** - Bones meet but cannot move relative to each other; found in the skull.
5. **Saddle Joints** - Allow movement in an oval pattern, seen in thumb movements.
6. **Condyloid Joints** - Permit back-and-forth and side-to-side motion, such as knuckle joints (connected to hand bones).
7. **Gliding Joints** - Bones move relative to each other through ligaments, found in wrists, ankles, and foot bones.

For example:
```java

    public static void main(String[] args) {
        System.out.println("Hinge Joints: Example of a door hinge or book binding.");
        System.out.println("Ball and Socket Joints: Shoulder or hip joint that allows wide range of motion.");
        System.out.println("Pivot Joints: Neck’s C1 and C2 vertebrae allowing head rotation.");
        // More joints can be added similarly
    }

Polyunsaturated fats are necessary but cannot be produced by the body.

Polyunsaturated fats are required but cannot be produced by the body and must be obtained through diet.
