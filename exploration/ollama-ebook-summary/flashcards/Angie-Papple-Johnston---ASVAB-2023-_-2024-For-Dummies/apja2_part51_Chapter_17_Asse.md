#### Mirroring vs. Rotation
Background context: The Assembling Objects subtest involves identifying correct shapes and their transformations, such as mirroring or rotation. Understanding these differences is crucial for solving problems accurately.

:p How do mirroring and rotation differ when repositioning a shape?
??x
Mirroring (flipping) and rotation are distinct operations on shapes. Mirroring creates a mirror image of the original shape, while rotation involves turning the shape around a central point without flipping it. For example, if you flip a jigsaw puzzle piece upside down, it might still fit but is not correctly placed. Similarly, rotating the same piece will change its orientation but keep its design intact.

Example:
- Rotating a triangle 90 degrees clockwise.
- Mirroring a triangle across a vertical axis to create an inverted mirror image.

These operations should be distinguished because the correct solution must match both the shape and its transformation accurately.
