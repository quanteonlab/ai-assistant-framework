#### pH Scale and Acid-Base Chemistry
Background context explaining the concept. Include any relevant formulas or data here.

The pH scale is a logarithmic scale used to specify the acidity or basicity of an aqueous solution. The range extends from 0 (most acidic) to 14 (most basic), with pure water having a neutral pH of 7. A change in whole pH values corresponds to a tenfold change in hydrogen ion concentration.

Formula:$\text{pH} = -\log_{10}[H^+]$ Acids give up positively charged hydrogen ions ($H^+$) when dissolved in water, while bases give up negatively charged hydroxyl ions ($ OH^-$). Pure water is neutral (pH 7), and substances that are more acidic or basic move further away from this value.

:p What does the pH scale measure, and what is its range?
??x
The pH scale measures the acidity or basicity of a solution. It ranges from 0 to 14.

#### Weight vs. Mass
Background context explaining the concept. Include any relevant formulas or data here.

Weight and mass are often confused but are distinct concepts:
- **Weight**: The force exerted by gravity on an object, measured in newtons (N). It changes with gravitational acceleration.
- **Mass**: A measure of matter's inertial property; it remains constant regardless of location.

Formula: $\text{Weight} = m \times g $ Where$m $ is mass and$g $ is the gravitational acceleration ($9.81\, m/s^2$ on Earth).

:p How do weight and mass differ in physics?
??x
Weight is the force exerted by gravity on an object, measured in newtons (N), while mass is a measure of matter's inertial property, remaining constant regardless of location.

#### Newton's First Law of Motion: The Law of Inertia
Background context explaining the concept. Include any relevant formulas or data here.
Sir Isaac Newton formulated his first law of motion, also known as the law of inertia, which states that an object at rest stays at rest and an object in motion stays in motion with the same speed and direction unless acted upon by unbalanced forces.

Mathematically, this can be understood through the principle of inertia:
- If the net force (sum of all external forces) acting on an object is zero, then the acceleration $a$ of that object will also be zero. This means no change in velocity.
- $\sum F = 0 \implies a = 0$:p What does Newton's first law of motion state?

Newton's first law of motion states that an object at rest stays at rest and an object in motion stays in motion with the same speed and direction unless acted upon by unbalanced forces.

#### Newton’s Second Law of Motion: Acceleration and Force Relationship
Background context explaining the concept. Include any relevant formulas or data here.

Newton's second law states that the acceleration of an object depends directly upon the net force acting on the object, and inversely upon the mass of the object. The mathematical expression is:
- $F = ma$ Where:
- $F$ is the net force
- $m$ is the mass of the object
- $a$ is the acceleration

:p What does Newton's second law state?

Newton's second law states that the acceleration ($a $) of an object is directly proportional to the net force ($ F $) acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass ($ m $). Mathematically, this can be expressed as$ F = ma$.
