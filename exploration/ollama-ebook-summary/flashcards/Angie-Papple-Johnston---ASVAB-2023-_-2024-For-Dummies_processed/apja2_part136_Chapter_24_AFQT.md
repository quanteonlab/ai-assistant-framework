#### Percentage Increase
Background context: This problem involves calculating a percentage increase based on current and previous values.

:p A tanning-bed pass for unlimited tanning costs $53 per month this year, but was only$50 per month last year. What is the percentage of increase?
??x
The formula to find the percentage increase is:
$$\text{Percentage Increase} = \left( \frac{\text{New Value} - \text{Old Value}}{\text{Old Value}} \right) \times 100$$

Given that the new value is $53 and the old value is$50:
$$\text{Percentage Increase} = \left( \frac{53 - 50}{50} \right) \times 100 = \left( \frac{3}{50} \right) \times 100 = 6\%$$

So, the answer is (C) 6 percent.
