#### Direct Proportions
Direct proportions occur when two sets of numbers increase or decrease at the same rate. You can identify them using the symbol :: or ∝.
:p How do you solve a direct proportion problem?
??x
To solve a direct proportion problem, set up the ratios as fractions and cross-multiply to find the unknown value. For example, if 1 inch represents 250 miles, then setting up the proportion $\frac{1}{250} = \frac{5}{x}$ and solving for x would give you the distance represented by 5 inches.

#### Rates
Rates are fixed quantities that can represent speed or money paid based on another amount. Relevant formulas include simple interest and distance calculations.
:p What is the formula for calculating simple interest?
??x

- $I$ represents the amount of interest,
- $P$ is the principal (initial amount invested),
- $r$ is the interest rate written as a decimal, and
- $t$ is the length of time in years the money is invested.

#### Distance Formula
The distance formula calculates how far something has traveled at a given speed over a period of time.
:p What is the formula for calculating distance?
??x
The distance formula is $d = rt$, where:
- $d$ represents the distance traveled,
- $r$ is the rate (speed) of travel, and
- $t$ is the amount of time traveled.
For example, if a car travels at 60 miles per hour for 2 hours, the distance it covers would be $d = 60 \times 2 = 120$ miles.

#### Converting to Scientific Notation
Scientific notation is a shorthand for very large or small numbers, consisting of two parts: 
- A number between 1 and 10
- A power of ten

For example:
$$125 = 1.25 \times 10^2$$
$$0.0000000579 = 5.79 \times 10^{-8}$$:p How do you convert a number to scientific notation?
??x
Move the decimal point in the original number so that it is after the first non-zero digit, and count how many places you moved it. The exponent on 10 will be positive if the movement was right or negative if left. 
For example:
$$125 \rightarrow 1.25 \times 10^2$$
$$0.0000000579 \rightarrow 5.79 \times 10^{-8}$$

#### Square Roots and Perfect Squares
A square root of a number is the factor that, when squared, equals the original number.
Perfect squares like 16 ($4^2 $), 25 ($5^2$), etc., have exact roots. Other numbers result in irrational numbers with non-repeating decimal expansions.

:p What are perfect squares and how do you find their square roots?
??x
Perfect squares are whole numbers that are the product of an integer squared, such as 16 (4²), 25 (5²). You can often identify them by memorizing the squares up to 12: 
$$\begin{align*}
1^2 &= 1 \\
2^2 &= 4 \\
3^2 &= 9 \\
4^2 &= 16 \\
\end{align*}$$
To find a square root without a calculator, estimate between known perfect squares and test. For example:
- Between 49 ($7^2 $) and 64 ($8^2$), the square root of 54 is approximately 7.3.

#### Estimating Square Roots of Non-Perfect Squares
For numbers that aren’t perfect squares, estimate by finding nearest perfect squares and testing with guesses.

:p How do you find the square root of a non-perfect number to the nearest tenth?
??x
Identify the nearest lower and higher perfect squares. Test values between these until close enough:
1. For 54: $\sqrt{49} = 7, \sqrt{64} = 8$. Try 7.3 and 7.4.
2. Calculate $7.3^2 = 53.29, 7.4^2 = 54.76$.
3. Since 53.29 is closer to 54 than 54.76, the root of 54 to one decimal place is 7.3.
