#### Richter Scale
Background context: The magnitude of an earthquake is measured on the Richter scale, which uses base 10 logarithmic scale. An increase by one unit on the Richter scale indicates a tenfold increase in measured amplitude.

:p An earthquake that measures 4 on the Richter scale would be how many times stronger than an earthquake that measured 2?
??x
The correct answer is (D) 100 times stronger.
On the Richter scale, each unit represents a tenfold increase in the amplitude of the seismic waves. Therefore, an earthquake with a magnitude of 4 is not just twice as strong but $10^{(4-2)} = 10^2 = 100$ times stronger than one that measures 2.

Formula:
$$\text{Ratio} = 10^{(\text{Magnitude}_1 - \text{Magnitude}_2)}$$

For example, for magnitudes 4 and 2:
$$10^{(4-2)} = 10^2 = 100$$

This means the energy released by an earthquake of magnitude 4 is 100 times greater than that of a magnitude 2.
