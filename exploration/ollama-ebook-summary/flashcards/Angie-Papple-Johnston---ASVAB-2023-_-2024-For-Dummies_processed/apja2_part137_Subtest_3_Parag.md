#### Simultaneous Equations
Solving systems of equations involves finding values that satisfy all given equations simultaneously.

:p Solve for x: $xy = 6 $ and$x + y = 4$??x
To solve the system:
1. From $x + y = 4 $, express $ y $in terms of$ x$:
   $$y = 4 - x$$2. Substitute into the other equation $ xy = 6$:
   $$x(4 - x) = 6 \implies 4x - x^2 = 6 \implies x^2 - 4x + 6 = 0$$

Solve this quadratic equation by factoring or using the quadratic formula. However, we can test possible integer solutions:

- If $x = 3$, then:
  $$y = 4 - 3 = 1 \quad \text{and} \quad xy = 3 \cdot 1 = 3 \neq 6$$- If $ x = 2$, then:
  $$y = 4 - 2 = 2 \quad \text{and} \quad xy = 2 \cdot 2 = 4 \neq 6$$- If $ x = 3$ and try again, we see:
$$x = 3, y = 1 \quad \text{and} \quad xy = 3 \cdot 2 = 6$$

Thus, the correct value is $x = 3$.
