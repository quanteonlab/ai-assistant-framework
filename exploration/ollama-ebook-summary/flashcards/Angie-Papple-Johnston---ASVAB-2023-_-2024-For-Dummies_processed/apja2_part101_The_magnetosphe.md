#### Earth's Atmosphere Layers
Background context: The atmosphere consists of several layers that vary in composition and function. Each layer serves distinct purposes in supporting life and regulating climate.

:p List the main layers of the Earth's atmosphere from closest to farthest from the surface.
??x
The main layers of the Earth's atmosphere, starting from the closest to the surface:

1. **Troposphere**: Extends between 6 and 12 miles above Earth and is higher at the equator than at the poles. This layer contains the jet stream and almost all weather changes occur here.
2. **Stratosphere**: Extends about 30 miles, with a major issue being ozone depletion due to chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs).
3. **Mesosphere**: Extends about 50 miles, where meteors burn up as they collide with gas particles.
4. **Ionosphere**: Extends about 70 miles and is critical for radio communications but may be considered part of the thermosphere by some scientists.
5. **Thermosphere**: Extends about 350 miles, home to the International Space Station’s orbit.
6. **Exosphere**: Extends about 6,200 miles, where atmospheric gases can escape into outer space.

The exact boundaries between layers and their definitions are subject to scientific debate.

#### Temperature Conversion Example
Background context: An easier way to remember conversions between Fahrenheit and Celsius is by using a systematic approach.

:p Can you explain the quick method for converting temperatures from one scale to another?
??x
To convert from Fahrenheit to Celsius, follow these steps:
1. Add 40 to the temperature in °F.
2. Multiply this sum by $\frac{5}{9}$.
3. Subtract 40.

For example, to convert 68°F to Celsius: 
1. $68 + 40 = 108 $2.$108 \times \frac{5}{9} = 60$
3. The result is 60°C.

To convert from Celsius to Fahrenheit, follow these steps:
1. Add 40 to the temperature in °C.
2. Multiply this sum by $\frac{9}{5}$.
3. Subtract 40.

For example, to convert 20°C to Fahrenheit:
1. $20 + 40 = 60 $2.$60 \times \frac{9}{5} = 108$
3. The result is 108°F.
