#### Card 2: Cardiac Muscle
Card context: Understanding cardiac muscle's unique properties and functions.

:p What are the characteristics of cardiac muscle, and how does it function in the human body?
??x
Cardiac muscle is unique because:
- It is found only in the heart.
- The fibers interlock with each other, allowing for coordinated contractions.
- It contracts involuntarily, meaning it works without conscious control.

This involuntary contraction allows your heart to pump blood continuously, ensuring that all parts of the body receive oxygen and nutrients. The cardiac muscle's ability to contract involuntarily is due to its specialized cells containing calcium ions that trigger contractions when electrical impulses are sent through the heart tissue.

#### Card 4: Skeletal Muscle
Card context: Understanding skeletal muscle's structure, functions, and control.

:p What are the characteristics of skeletal muscles, and how do they differ from other types of muscles?
??x
Skeletal muscles are characterized by:
- Voluntary contraction, meaning their movement is controlled consciously.
- Striated appearance due to organized fibers under a microscope.
- Cylindrical shape with multiple nuclei per cell.

These muscles primarily function in voluntary movements such as running, jumping, and controlling body posture. Unlike cardiac and smooth muscles, skeletal muscles can be consciously controlled to perform specific actions like lifting weights or pointing at an object.

#### Card 6: Bone Formation - Ossification
Card context: Understanding the process of bone formation from cartilage.

:p What is ossification, and how does it transform cartilage into bone?
??x
Ossification is the process by which cartilage transforms into bone. This process involves:
1. Calcified matrix deposition: Calcium and phosphorus are deposited in the matrix.
2. Bone cell differentiation: Osteoblasts (bone-forming cells) differentiate into osteocytes, filling the spaces between newly formed bone.

The transformation from cartilage to bone is critical for the structural integrity of bones.

#### Card 7: Types of Joints
Card context: Understanding the different types of joints in the human body and their functions.

:p What are the seven major types of joints found in the human body, and what do they enable?
??x
The seven major types of joints are:
1. **Hinge Joints**: Like elbows and knees (similar to a door hinge).
2. **Ball and Socket Joints**: Such as shoulders and hips (allows wide range of motion like a joystick).
3. **Pivot Joints**: In the neck's first and second vertebrae, allowing head rotation.
4. **Fixed Joints**: Where bones meet with no movement capability, such as skull joints.
5. **Saddle Joints**: Allow oval motion, like thumb movements.
6. **Condyloid Joints**: Permit back-and-forth and side-to-side movement in knuckles (fingers).
7. **Gliding Joints**: Found between surfaces held by ligaments, e.g., wrists, ankles.

These joints enable various types of movement crucial for daily activities.

#### Human Reproduction Process
For reproduction, a male's sperm must fertilize a female's ovum. Women typically release an egg from their ovaries every 28 days; if it is not fertilized, the lining of the uterus sheds as menstruation. If fertilization occurs, the egg becomes a zygote and may implant in the uterine wall.
:p What triggers the release of an egg from the ovary?
??x
A woman releases an egg (ovum) approximately every 28 days due to hormonal cycles triggered by the hypothalamus and pituitary gland. These hormones stimulate follicle development, leading to ovulation when a mature egg is released.
