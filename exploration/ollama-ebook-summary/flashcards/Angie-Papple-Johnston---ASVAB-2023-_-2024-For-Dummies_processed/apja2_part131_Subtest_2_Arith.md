#### Profit Calculation on Donuts
Background context: The problem involves calculating profit from selling donuts and understanding cost, revenue, and profit relationships.

:p A baker sells a dozen donuts for $3.99. The cost to make three donuts is$0.45. How much is the total profit on 5 dozen donuts?
??x
To find the total profit, first calculate the selling price per donut and then determine the cost per donut.

1. Selling price per dozen = $3.99.
2. Cost for three donuts = $0.45, so cost per donut =$0.45 / 3 =$0.15.
3. Cost for a dozen (12) donuts = 12 * $0.15 =$1.80.
4. Revenue from selling one dozen = $3.99, and the cost is$1.80.
5. Profit per dozen = $3.99 -$1.80 =$2.19.
6. Total profit for 5 dozen donuts = 5 * $2.19 =$10.95.

Therefore, the total profit on 5 dozen donuts is **$10.95**.

#### Coin Calculation
Background context: The problem involves determining the number of dimes in a piggy bank containing both dimes and quarters with a given total value and quantity.

:p Your piggy bank contains $19.75 in dimes and quarters. There are 100 coins in all. How many dimes are there?
??x
Let $d $ be the number of dimes, and$q$ be the number of quarters.

From the problem:
1. Total value:$0.10d + 0.25q = 19.75$.
2. Total coins: $d + q = 100$.

We can solve these equations by substitution or elimination. Let's use substitution:

- From the second equation, we get $q = 100 - d$.
- Substitute into the first equation:
  $$0.10d + 0.25(100 - d) = 19.75.$$

Simplify and solve for $d$:
$$0.10d + 25 - 0.25d = 19.75,$$
$$-0.15d = -5.25,$$
$$d = \frac{5.25}{0.15} = 35.$$

Therefore, there are **35 dimes**.
