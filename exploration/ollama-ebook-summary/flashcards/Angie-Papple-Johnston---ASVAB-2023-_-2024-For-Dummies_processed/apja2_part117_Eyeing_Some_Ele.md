#### Wire Color Codes in US Electrical Systems
Background context: In the United States, electrical wires follow specific color standards for identification and safety. These codes help electricians distinguish between different types of wires (phase, neutral, ground) during installations or repairs.

:p What are the standard color codes for single-phase and phase 2 wires?
??x
The standard color codes for single-phase and phase 2 wires in U.S. electrical systems are as follows:

- **Single-phase wire**:
  - Black: Phase 1
  - Red: Not applicable (single-phase has only two hot wires)
  - Blue: Neutral
  - White: Neutral

- **Phase 2 wire**:
  - Black: Not applicable (two live wires, each providing the same voltage but with different peaks and dips in voltage)
  - Red: Live (one of the phase 2 live wires)
  - Blue: Neutral
  - Green or bare: Ground

??x
The answer includes a detailed explanation:

- **Single-phase wire**: 
  - Black is used for Phase 1, while red is not typically used in single-phase systems.
  - White signifies neutral, which is common across both single and phase 2 wires.

- **Phase 2 wire**:
  - Black is also not applicable here as it’s a two-wire system with no distinction by color. 
  - Red represents one of the live (phase) wires, while blue still signifies the neutral.
  - Green or bare wire indicates ground, ensuring safety in electrical installations.
