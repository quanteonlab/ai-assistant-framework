#### Newton's Second Law of Motion
Background context: Newton’s second law of motion states that the acceleration (a) of an object as produced by a net force (F) is directly proportional to the magnitude of the net force, in the same direction as the net force, and inversely proportional to the mass (m) of the object. The formula is expressed as $\mathbf{F} = m\mathbf{a}$. To find the net force, you can rearrange this equation:$\mathbf{F} = m\mathbf{a}$.

#### Work Calculation
Background context explaining the formula for work and its application. The formula for work is $W = F \times d \times \cos(\theta)$, where $ W$is work,$ F $is force,$ d $ is distance, and $\theta$ is the angle between the force and displacement vectors.
:p How do you calculate the work done when exerting a force?
??x
To calculate the work done, use the formula $W = F \times d \times \cos(\theta)$. This accounts for the component of the force that acts in the direction of the displacement. For example, if a 10 N force is applied over a distance of 5 meters at an angle of 30 degrees to the direction of motion, the work done would be $ W = 10 \times 5 \times \cos(30^\circ) \approx 43.3 \text{ Joules}$.
