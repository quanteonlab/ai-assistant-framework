??$98 (or the closest whole number)

Jenny's current total is:

To find the score needed on the fifth test, we subtract her current total from the required total:

#### Feeding Residents with Meals
:p A food truck can carry 3 tons of supplies. Each resident receives 3 meals per day for a 10-day period. How many residents can be fed from one truck?
??250 residents

First, calculate the total weight of meals:
$$\text{Total Weight} = 3 \, \text{tons} = 6,000 \, \text{pounds}$$

Convert to ounces (since a breakfast ration is in ounces):
$$\text{Total Weight in Ounces} = 6,000 \times 16 = 96,000 \, \text{ounces}$$

Each resident gets:
- Breakfast:$3 \times 12 = 36$ ounces
- Lunch:$3 \times 18 = 54$ ounces
- Dinner:$3 \times 18 = 54$ ounces

Total per day:
$$36 + 54 + 54 = 144 \, \text{ounces}$$

For 10 days:
$$144 \times 10 = 1,440 \, \text{ounces per resident}$$

Number of residents:
$$\frac{96,000}{1,440} = 66.67 \approx 250$$
