#### Levels
Background context: Levels are used to determine if a surface is horizontal or vertical. They contain small tubes filled with liquid and an air bubble that indicates when the surface is true.

:p How does a torpedo level work?
??x
A torpedo level works by placing it on a surface, where one or more small tubes containing alcohol or another liquid along with an air bubble will show if the surface is level. When the air bubble remains exactly in the center of the tube(s), the surface is true and level.

#### Squares
Background context: Squares are used to check trueness of angles and can also be used for measuring. They have two arms—a long one (the blade) and a short one (the tongue)—that meet at a right angle.

:p How do you use a square?
??x
You use a square by setting its blade against the surface where an angle should measure 90 degrees. If there is any gap between the square and the material, it indicates that the material isn't true or isn't at 90 degrees.
