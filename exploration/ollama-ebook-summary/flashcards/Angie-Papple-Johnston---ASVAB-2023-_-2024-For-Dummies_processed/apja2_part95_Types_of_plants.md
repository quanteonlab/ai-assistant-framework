#### Plant Cells vs. Animal Cells
Background context explaining differences between plant and animal cells. Plant cells have additional structures like cell walls, larger vacuoles, chloroplasts, and lack centrioles and lysosomes.

:p How do plant cells differ from animal cells?
??x
Plant cells differ from animal cells in several ways:
1. Cell wall - supports and protects the cell.
2. Larger vacuoles - storage areas not found in animal cells.
3. Chloroplasts - contain chlorophyll for photosynthesis, absent in animal cells.
4. Absence of centrioles (cylindrical structures involved in cell division).
5. Lack of lysosomes (sacs of enzymes) found in plant cells.

#### Mitosis vs Meiosis
Mitosis is the process by which a cell divides to produce identical daughter cells, each containing the same number of chromosomes. In contrast, meiosis produces sex cells (eggs and sperm) with half the number of chromosomes.

:p What are the two main types of cell division in genetics?
??x
The two main types of cell division in genetics are mitosis and meiosis.
- Mitosis results in daughter cells that have identical sets of 46 chromosomes.
- Meiosis results in four haploid (23 chromosomes) gametes.
