The effort required to move an object up an inclined plane is less than lifting the same object vertically. The force needed can be calculated using the formula $F_{\text{effort}} = \frac{W}{\sin(\theta)}$, where $ W$is the weight of the object and $\theta$ is the angle of the incline.

:p To move a 400-pound crate from the floor to a truck bed that is 4 feet off the ground, what device should be used for the most efficient effort?
??x
The most efficient device to use in this scenario would be an inclined plane. The effort required can be calculated using $F_{\text{effort}} = \frac{W}{\sin(\theta)}$, where $ W$is 400 pounds and $\theta$ is the angle of the incline (which depends on the length and height).
