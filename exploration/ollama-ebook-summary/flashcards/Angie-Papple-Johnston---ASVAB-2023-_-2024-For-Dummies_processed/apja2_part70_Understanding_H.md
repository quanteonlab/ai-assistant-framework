#### Military Branches and Line Scores
Background context explaining how each branch uses line scores differently: Army guarantees a job, Air Force offers guaranteed jobs or aptitude areas, Coast Guard enlists undesignated seamen with general work roles, Marine Corps provides guaranteed job fields but not specific MOSs until basic training, and Navy has most recruits enlist with guaranteed jobs.
:p How do the different branches use line scores to determine military jobs?
??x
The different branches use line scores in various ways:
- **Army**: Guarantees a job for all new recruits (except infantry or Special Forces).
- **Air Force**: About 40% of active duty Air Force recruits enlist with a guaranteed job, while the majority enlists in one of four guaranteed aptitude areas.
- **Coast Guard**: Rarely offers a guaranteed job; new recruits start as undesignated seamen and apply for specific training later.
- **Marine Corps**: Guarantees jobs through MOSs but often not until halfway through basic training.
- **Navy**: Most recruits enlist with a guaranteed job, with some striking (applying) for specific positions after boot camp.

#### Marine Corps Line Scores
The Marine Corps uses a system of line scores to assess job qualification, derived from various ASVAB subtests. These line scores are used for determining specific military jobs.

To calculate each line score, the Marine Corps adds up standard scores from different ASVAB subtests as shown in Table 2-3:

- **Mechanical Line Score**: Uses General Science (GS), Auto & Shop Information (AS), Mathematics Knowledge (MK), and Mechanical Comprehension (MC) subtest scores.
  - Formula: GS + AS + MK + MC

- **General Technical Line Score**: Uses Verbal Expression (VE) and Arithmetic Reasoning (AR) subtest scores.
  - Formula: VE + AR

- **Electronics Line Score**: Uses General Science (GS), Arithmetic Reasoning (AR), Mathematics Knowledge (MK), and Electronics Information (EI) subtest scores.
  - Formula: GS + AR + MK + EI

:p How is the Mechanical line score calculated in the Marine Corps?
??x
The Mechanical line score is calculated by adding the standard scores from General Science, Auto & Shop Information, Mathematics Knowledge, and Mechanical Comprehension subtests. This sum gives an overall measure of a recruit's mechanical aptitude.
```java
int GS = 75;
int AS = 80;
int MK = 90;
int MC = 85;

int mechanicalLineScore = GS + AS + MK + MC; // Example calculation
```

#### Air Force MAGE Scores
The U.S. Air Force uses the ASVAB to derive four aptitude areas called MAGE (Mechanical, Administrative, General, and Electronics), which are calculated as percentiles.

To calculate each MAGE score, specific subtests are combined into a formula:

- **Mechanical Line Score**: Uses General Science (GS), Mechanical Comprehension (MC), and twice the Auto & Shop Information (AS) subtest scores.
  - Formula: GS + MC + 2 * AS

- **Administrative Line Score**: Uses Numerical Operations (NO), Coding Speed (CS), and Verbal Expression (VE) subtest scores.
  - Formula: NO + CS + VE

- **General Line Score**: Uses Arithmetic Reasoning (AR) and Verbal Expression (VE) subtest scores.
  - Formula: AR + VE

- **Electronics Line Score**: Uses General Science (GS), Arithmetic Reasoning (AR), Mathematics Knowledge (MK), and Electronics Information (EI) subtest scores.
  - Formula: GS + AR + MK + EI

:p How is the Mechanical MAGE score calculated in the Air Force?
??x
The Mechanical MAGE score is calculated by adding the standard scores from General Science, Mechanical Comprehension, and twice the Auto & Shop Information subtests. This sum is then converted to a percentile.
```java
int GS = 75;
int MC = 80;
int AS = 85;

int mechanicalMAGEScore = GS + MC + 2 * AS; // Example calculation
```

#### AFQT Score
The Armed Forces Qualification Test (AFQT) score is a critical measure used by the military to determine overall enlistment qualification. It's derived from the math and English subtests of the ASVAB.

To calculate the AFQT score, specific subtest scores are combined:

- **Subtests Used**: Arithmetic Reasoning (AR), Mathematics Knowledge (MK), Verbal Expression (VE), and Paragraph Comprehension (PC).
  - Formula: 2 * AR + MK + VE + PC

:p What is the formula for calculating the AFQT score?
??x
The AFQT score is calculated using a specific formula that combines scores from four ASVAB subtests. The formula is:
$$\text{AFQT} = 2 \times \text{AR} + \text{MK} + \text{VE} + \text{PC}$$
This provides an overall measure of the recruit's readiness for military service.
```java
int AR = 70;
int MK = 85;
int VE = 90;
int PC = 65;

int afqtScore = 2 * AR + MK + VE + PC; // Example calculation
```
