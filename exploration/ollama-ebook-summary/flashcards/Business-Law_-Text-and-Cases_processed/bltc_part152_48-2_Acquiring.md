#### Definition of Bailment
Bailments involve the temporary delivery of personal property from one person (bailor) to another (bailee) without transferring title. The bailee is expected to return the property or dispose of it as directed after fulfilling a specific purpose, such as storing, repairing, or transporting.
:p What is a bailment?
??x
A bailment occurs when personal property is delivered from one person (bailor) to another (bailee) without transferring title. The bailee must return the property in the same condition or better upon completion of the purpose agreed upon, such as storing, repairing, or transporting.
x??

#### Elements of a Bailment
For a transaction involving the delivery of personal property to be considered a bailment, three elements must be present: 1) Personal property, 2) Delivery of possession (without title), and 3) Agreement for return or disposal according to the owner's directions.
:p What are the three elements required for a bailment?
??x
The three elements required for a bailment are:
1. Personal property—must be tangible or intangible but not real property or persons.
2. Delivery of possession (without title)—the bailee must have exclusive control and knowingly accept the property.
3. Agreement for return or disposal according to the owner's directions.
