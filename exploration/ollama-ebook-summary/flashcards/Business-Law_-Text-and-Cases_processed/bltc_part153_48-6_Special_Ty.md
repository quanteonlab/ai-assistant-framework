#### Land and Structures
Land consists of the soil on its surface and any natural or artificial structures attached to it. It also includes waters contained on or under its surface and a significant portion of the airspace above it.
:p What does land include according to this text?
??x
Land includes the soil, natural products, artificial structures attached to it, waters contained on or below its surface, and a significant part of the airspace above it. The exterior boundaries extend down into the earth and up to the atmosphere.

#### Airspace Rights
Ownership rights in real property include both airspace above the land and subsurface rights underneath it. Limitations on these rights are called encumbrances and must be indicated when transferring title.
:p What rights does the owner of real property have regarding airspace?
??x
The owner of real property has the right to use and control the airspace above their land, subject to limitations or encumbrances that must be noted during a sale. These limitations can affect commercial and private planes flying over, cloud seeding by individuals or governments, and other activities.
x??

#### Subsurface Rights
Subsurface rights can be separated from surface ownership in many states. An owner can sell subsurface rights to another without needing the consent of the surface owner. The value lies in owning minerals, oil, or natural gas, but this right is only useful if it includes access to the surface.
:p Can subsurface and surface rights be owned separately?
??x
Yes, in many states, ownership of land can be separated into surface and subsurface rights. This means one can sell subsurface rights without the consent of the surface owner. The value lies in owning minerals, oil, or natural gas beneath the surface, but this right is meaningful only if it includes access to the surface.
