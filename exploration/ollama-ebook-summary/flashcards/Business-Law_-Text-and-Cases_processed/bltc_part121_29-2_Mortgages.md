#### Suretyship and Guaranty Relationships
Suretyship involves a third party agreeing to be responsible for another's debt, while a guarantor agrees to be secondarily responsible. A surety is primarily liable from the moment the debt becomes due, whereas a guarantor’s liability kicks in only if the principal debtor defaults.
:p What are the key differences between a surety and a guarantor?
??x
A surety is primarily liable for the debt immediately when it comes due, while a guarantor is secondarily liable, meaning they step in only after the primary debtor has defaulted. The creditor can demand payment from either party without exhausting all legal remedies against the principal debtor.
```java
// Example of a suretyship and guaranty relationship
public class SuretyExample {
    public void handleDebt(String debtor, String surety, String guarantor) {
        // Assume conditions for default are met
        if (debtor == "defaulted") {
            System.out.println("Requesting payment from both surety: " + surety + " and guarantor: " + guarantor);
        } else {
            System.out.println("Debtor has not defaulted yet.");
        }
    }
}
```

#### Strict Suretyship Agreement
In a strict suretyship, the third party (surety) is primarily liable for the principal debtor's obligations. The creditor can demand payment directly from the surety without exhausting legal remedies against the principal.

??x
A strict suretyship agreement makes the surety primarily liable to the creditor, allowing the creditor to seek payment directly from the surety as soon as the debt becomes due. The surety cannot defer payments until all possible actions are taken against the principal debtor.

#### Rights of Subrogation for Sureties

Background context: When a surety pays the debt owed to the creditor, they acquire certain rights known as subrogation. This means that any rights the creditor had against the debtor now become the rights of the surety.

:p What is the right of subrogation in a suretyship arrangement?
??x
The surety has the legal right to take over the position of the creditor and pursue any remedies available to the creditor against the debtor. This includes rights in bankruptcy, rights to collateral, and rights to judgments.
??

#### Right of Reimbursement for Sureties

Background context: After paying a debt on behalf of a principal (debtor), a surety has the right to be reimbursed by the debtor for all outlays made under the suretyship arrangement. These can include expenses as well as the actual amount paid to the creditor.

:p What is the right of reimbursement in a suretyship agreement?
??x
The surety is entitled to receive from the debtor all outlays made on behalf of the suretyship, including both expenses and the actual amount of the debt paid to the creditor.

#### Right of Contribution for Sureties

Background context: When two or more sureties are involved (co-sureties), a co-surety who pays more than their proportionate share due to another co-surety's default has the right of contribution. This means the overpaid co-surety can recover from other co-sureties the amount paid in excess of their obligation.

:p What is the right of contribution for sureties?
??x
Co-sureties have a right of contribution when one pays more than its proportionate share due to another's default. The overpaid co-surety can recover this excess payment from other co-sureties.
