#### Liability for Negligence in Professional Services
Background context: Accountants and other professionals can be held liable under common law for negligence. To establish negligence, a plaintiff must prove four elements: duty, breach, causation, and damages. In professional contexts, particularly with accountants, the focus is often on the standard of care exercised.
:p What are the four elements required to establish negligence in professional services?
??x
The four elements required to establish negligence in professional services are:
1. Duty: A legal duty exists between the professional and the client.
2. Breach: The professional breached that duty by failing to act as a reasonably prudent person would have under similar circumstances.
3. Causation: There is a direct causal link between the breach and the harm suffered by the client.
4. Damages: The client suffered actual damages due to the breach.

Explanation: Each element must be proven for negligence to be established in a professional context, especially with accountants who handle confidential data like client information stored on cloud platforms.
