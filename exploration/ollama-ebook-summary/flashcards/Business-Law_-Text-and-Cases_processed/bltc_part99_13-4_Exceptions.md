#### Minors and Voidable Contracts
Minors have the right to disaffirm (void) contracts they enter into, provided that they can clearly show an intention not to be bound by it. This is subject to certain exceptions where courts may find in favor of the other party.
:p What are the conditions under which a minor can void a contract?
??x
A minor can void a contract if they:
1. Manifest (clearly show) an intention not to be bound by it through words or actions.
2. Disaffirm the entire contract, not just part of it.
3. Do so before reaching adulthood.
If a court determines that disaffirmance is not in the minor's best interest, they may still be required to fulfill their contractual obligations.
