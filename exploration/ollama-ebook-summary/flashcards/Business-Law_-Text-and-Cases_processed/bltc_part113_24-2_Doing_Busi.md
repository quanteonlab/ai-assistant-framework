#### Act of State Doctrine
The Act of State Doctrine is a principle that U.S. courts will avoid passing judgment on the validity of public acts committed by a recognized foreign government within its own territory. This doctrine ensures that internal governmental actions are not subject to scrutiny from foreign legal systems.

The doctrine addresses the situation where U.S. courts do not intervene in matters involving public acts performed by a foreign state on its own soil, ensuring that such acts remain immune from judicial review.

Sovereign immunity refers to the principle under which a nation or its agencies are immune from certain types of legal action and jurisdiction within another country. The Foreign Sovereign Immunities Act (FSIA) sets out specific conditions under which foreign states can be sued in U.S. courts.
