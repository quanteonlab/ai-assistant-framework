In corporate governance, directors and officers must disclose any potential conflicts of interest they may have. This ensures transparency and maintains trust among shareholders.

:p What is required when a director or officer has a personal conflict of interest?

??x
When a director or officer has a personal conflict of interest, they are required to fully disclose the nature of the conflicting interest and all pertinent facts known to them regarding the transaction. They must also abstain from voting on any proposed transactions related to this conflict.

#### Liability of Directors and Officers

Directors and officers can face various liabilities, including negligence, crimes committed by their employees under supervision, or mismanagement perceived by shareholders.

:p What are the potential liabilities faced by directors and officers?

??x
Directors and officers may be held liable for several reasons:
1. **Negligence**: They can be sued if they fail to act with due care.
2. **Crimes and Torts**: They can be held responsible for crimes or torts committed by employees under their supervision.
3. **Shareholder Suits**: Shareholders can sue directors on behalf of the corporation if they believe the directors are not acting in the best interests of the company (known as a derivative suit).
4. **Statutory Liabilities**: They may be held personally liable for violations of certain statutes, such as consumer protection or environmental laws.

For example, if a director fails to prevent an employee from committing fraud, the director could be held liable under negligence and supervision principles.

#### Duties and Liabilities of Directors and Officers

Directors and officers have specific duties and can be held liable for various actions related to corporate governance.

:p What are the key duties and liabilities of directors and officers?

??x
Key duties and liabilities include:
1. **Duty of Care**: Directors must act in good faith, use prudent business judgment, and act in the corporation's best interests.
2. **Business Judgment Rule**: This rule protects directors from liability as long as their decisions are informed, reasonable, and loyal to the corporation.

#### Cumulative Voting Overview
Cumulative voting is a system that allows shareholders to cast their total number of votes, which equals the number of board members to be elected multiplied by the number of shares they own, for one or more candidates. This can be advantageous for minority shareholders who may have a better chance of electing at least one representative on the board.
:p How does cumulative voting work?
??x
Cumulative voting works by giving each shareholder a total number of votes equal to the number of board members to be elected multiplied by the number of shares they own. These votes can then be cast for one candidate or distributed among several nominees. For example, if three directors are to be elected and a shareholder owns 100 shares, they would have 300 votes (3 directors x 100 shares).
```java
// Example calculation in pseudocode
int totalVotes = numDirectors * numShares;
```
